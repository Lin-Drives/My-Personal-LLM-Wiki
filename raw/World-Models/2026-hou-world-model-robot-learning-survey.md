# World Model for Robot Learning: A Comprehensive Survey

> Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00080
> Collected: 2026-05-31
> Published: 2026-04-30

**Authors:** Bohan Hou, Gen Li, Jindou Jia, et al. (18 authors)

## Abstract

The paper defines world models as predictive representations of how environments evolve under actions. From a robot learning perspective, the survey systematically reviews how world models are coupled with robot policies, their role as learned simulators for reinforcement learning and evaluation, and the evolution of robotic video world models from imagination-based generation toward controllable, structured, and foundation-scale formulations. It also covers navigation, autonomous driving, datasets, benchmarks, and evaluation protocols. The survey proposes a three core capability framework: Foresight (future state prediction), Imagination (internal simulation and policy optimization), and Simulation (interactive environments for RL/imitation learning). A maintained GitHub repository accompanies the paper.
