# World-Ego Modeling for Long-Horizon Evolution in Hybrid Embodied Tasks

> Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.19957
> Collected: 2026-05-31
> Published: 2026-05-19

**Authors:** Zuyao Lin, Jianhui Zhang, Peidong Jia, Xiaoguang Zhao, Shanghang Zhang, Xingyu Chen

## Abstract

World models are widely used in embodied intelligence, but typically entanglement of world and ego evolution leads to degradation in long-horizon embodied scenarios. The authors propose World-Ego Modeling, a new paradigm that decomposes future evolution into world and ego components. They define the world-ego boundary from three perspectives and analyze three decoupling strategies. The paradigm is instantiated as WEM, a unified embodied world model containing an implicit world-ego planner and a CP-MoE diffusion generator. For rigorous evaluation, they construct HTEWorld, the first benchmark for long-horizon world modeling with 125K video clips (over 4.5 million frames) and fine-grained action annotations. Experiments show WEM achieves state-of-the-art performance on HTEWorld and remains competitive on pure manipulation benchmarks.
