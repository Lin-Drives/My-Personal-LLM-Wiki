# From Imagined Futures to Executable Actions: Mixture of Latent Actions for Robot Manipulation

> Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.12167
> Collected: 2026-05-31
> Published: 2026-05-12 (ICML 2026)

**Authors:** Yajie Li, Bozhou Zhang, Chun Gu, Zipei Ma, Jiahui Zhang, Jiankang Deng, Xiatian Zhu, Li Zhang

## Abstract

Using video generation models for robot manipulation faces a limitation: predicted future frames emphasize perceptual fidelity rather than action-centric causes of state transitions. MoLA (Mixture of Latent Actions) is a control-oriented interface that transforms imagined future videos into executable representations. It uses a mixture of pretrained inverse dynamics models to infer latent actions from generated visual transitions, capturing complementary semantic, depth, and flow cues. Evaluation on simulated benchmarks (LIBERO, CALVIN, and LIBERO-Plus) and real-world robot manipulation tasks shows improvements in task success, temporal consistency, and generalization. Accepted at ICML 2026.
