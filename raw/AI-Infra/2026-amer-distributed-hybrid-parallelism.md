# Distributed Hybrid Parallelism for Large Language Models: Comparative Study and System Design Guide

> Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.09109
> Collected: 2026-05-31
> Published: 2026-02-09

**Authors:** Hossam Amer, Rezaul Karim, Ali Pourranjbar, Weiwei Zhang, Walid Ahmed, Boxing Chen

## Abstract

A wide range of methods have been developed to distribute computation and memory across hardware devices for LLM training and inference. This paper offers a comprehensive review of collective operations and distributed parallel strategies, complemented by mathematical formulations. The authors examine hybrid parallelization designs, emphasizing communication computation overlap across different stages. They also discuss automated search for optimal hybrid parallelization strategies using cost models and present case studies with mainstream architecture categories to reveal empirical insights for strategy selection. Finally, they highlight open challenges and limitations of current LLM training paradigms.
