# M100: An Orchestrated Dataflow Architecture Powering General AI Computing

> Source: https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17862
> Collected: 2026-05-31
> Published: 2026-04-20 (ISCA 2026 Industry Track)

**Authors:** Yan Xie, Changkui Mao, Changsong Wu, et al. (37 authors, Li Auto)

## Abstract

Deep learning-based AI technologies have driven the need for general AI computing architectures. GPGPUs, while general-purpose, often fall short in efficiency and cost-effectiveness. M100 is Li Auto's response, targeting autonomous driving, large language models, and intelligent human-machine interaction. It adopts a dataflow parallel architecture, where compiler-architecture co-design orchestrates not only computation but, more critically, data movement across time and space. Through hardware-software co-design, it improves system performance while reducing hardware complexity and cost, and substantially eliminates caching mechanisms. Benchmarks demonstrate that M100 outperforms GPGPU architectures in AD applications, representing a promising direction for future general AI computing. Accepted at ISCA 2026 Industry Track.
